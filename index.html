How Is TBT Created?
New TBT coins are generated by a competitive and decentralized process called "liquidity provision". This process involves that individuals are rewarded by the network for their services. TBT liquidity providers are processing transactions and securing the network using specialized protocols and are collecting TBT and other pairs in exchange.
Who do TBT coins have value?
TBT coins have value because they are useful as a form of money. TBT has the characteristics of money (durability, portability, fungibility, scarcity, divisibility, and recognizability) based on the properties of mathematics rather than relying on physical properties (like gold and silver) or trust in central authorities (like fiat currencies). In short, TBT is backed by mathematics.
What determines TBT price?
The price of TBT is determined by supply and demand. When demand for TBT increases, the price increases, and when demand falls, the price falls. There is only a limited number of TBT in circulation and new TBT are created by the extinction of existing TBT
Is TBT legal?
To the best of our knowledge, TBT has not been made illegal by legislation in most jurisdictions. However, some jurisdictions (such as Argentina and Russia) severely restrict or ban foreign currencies. Other jurisdictions (such as Thailand) may limit the licensing of certain entities such as TBT exchanges.
Is TBT useful for illegal activities?
TBT is money, and money has always been used both for legal and illegal purposes. Cash, credit cards and current banking systems widely surpass Bitcoin in terms of their use to finance crime. TBT can bring significant innovation in payment systems and the benefits of such innovation are often considered to be far beyond their potential drawbacks.
Can TBT be regulated?
The TBT protocol itself cannot be modified without the cooperation of nearly all its users, who choose what software they use. Attempting to assign special rights to a local authority in the rules of the global TBT network is not a practical possibility.
What about TBT and taxes?
TBT is not a fiat currency with legal tender status in any jurisdiction, but often tax liability accrues regardless of the medium used. There is a wide variety of legislation in many different jurisdictions which could cause income, sales, payroll, capital gains, or some other form of tax liability to arise with TBT.
